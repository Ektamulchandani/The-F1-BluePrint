Reroot is a free, step-by-step guide for international students, from the moment you decide to apply to a US program, to the moment you sign a full-time offer. Built by someone who lived every step of it.

Free. No account needed. Takes 2 minutes to set up.
What Reroot covers
Phase 1
Getting In
Scholarships, program shortlisting, GRE, SOP, and letters of recommendation, anchored to real timelines
Phase 2
Choosing Your University
A rating tool to compare programs on the factors that actually matter, including CPT, career services, and alumni networks
Phase 3
Visa and Pre-Arrival
I-20, SEVIS fee, visa interview, housing, banking, and health insurance, before you land
Phase 4
First Semester
Career services, US resume standards, clubs, professors, LinkedIn, coffee chats, and CPT timeline
Phase 5
Internship Hunt
Referrals, CPT authorization, Handshake, behavioral interviews, and application tracking
Phase 6
Summer
Proving yourself, documenting deliverables, applying in parallel, and getting midpoint feedback
Phase 7
Third Semester
Leadership, full-time applications, H-1B filtering, interviewing, and locking in an offer
Phase 8
Final Semester
OPT filing, H-1B conversation, offer negotiation, commencement speaker, and passing it forward
Who this is for
This is for you if...
+You are an international student planning to study in the US
+You want to work in the US after graduation
+You are navigating CPT, OPT, or H-1B for the first time
+You have no US network and are building one from scratch
+You want a step-by-step guide from someone who did it
This is not for you if...
-You are a domestic student (some parts may still help)
-You are looking for immigration legal advice
-You are on a J-1 or other non-F-1 visa (some overlap, but this is built for F-1)
-You have already completed your degree and are fully employed
Built by
An international student from India who applied from abroad, arrived with no US network, landed internships on CPT, converted to a full-time role on OPT, and is now navigating H-1B sponsorship. No institution funded this. No consulting firm built it. It exists because no single resource like it existed when it was needed most.
